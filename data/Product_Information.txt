Investment Products
1. Bonds
Description: Bonds are long term debt instruments issued by corporates and governments to raise funds for capital projects.
Features: It is issued at Par, Discount or Premium. It is a tradable security. It is usually listed on the stock exchange. It provides fixed or floating coupon rates
Benefits: It is highly liquid and can be used as collateral for loans. Government bonds are tax-exempt. Government bonds are risk-free. Returns are usually higher than inflation. It provides multiple sources of income (Coupon is paid twice a year)

2. Commercial Papers
Description: It is an unsecured negotiable short-term instrument issued by a corporate, usually for financing short-term liabilities and working capital.
Features: It is a discount instrument. It usually offers higher yields than treasury bills because of credit risk premium. It has short maturities up to 270days. Interest earned is taxable.
Benefits: It usually offers higher yields than treasury bills because of credit risk premium.

3. Money Market Deposits
Description: These are cash placements with banks for a set period of time. It could be in the following form- Overnight deposit, Call deposit or Tenured deposit.
Features: It is interest earning. Interest earned is taxable. Premature liquidation attracts penalty.
Benefits: It offers higher interest rates and it is highly liquid.

4. Treasury Bills
Description: It is a short-term debt instrument issued by the Federal Government through the Central Bank to provide short term funding for the government.
Features: It is backed by the full faith and credit of the Federal Government of Nigeria. It is issued for tenors of 91 days, 182 days and 364 days at the primary market auction held fortnightly by CBN. Other tenors are available in the secondary market. Interest payment is upfront. It is a highly liquid instrument and can be used as collateral for loans. 
Benefits: It is risk-free. The interest earned is tax-exempt.
